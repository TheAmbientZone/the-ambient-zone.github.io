HAL 90026 · manual

A drone that tunes and plays itself. It holds a low root tone and keeps growing new tones tuned to it, so a slowly-shifting chord drifts on indefinitely. Nothing is recorded or uploaded — it all runs on your phone, offline.

How to use

Tap the eye to start. Pick a tuning system (top row), then set voices (how many tones at once), motion (how fast it evolves), and root (how low it sits). Tap a preset for an instant starting point. Tap stop to silence it.

How the microtonal drone works

A piano splits the octave into 12 equal steps — a compromise that leaves every interval slightly out of tune. This uses just intonation instead: each tone is a simple whole-number ratio of the root — 3/2, 5/4, 7/4 — which is exactly what the label on each glowing memory module shows.

When two tones are a pure ratio they lock together and the slow "beating" (that wah-wah) almost stops; complex ratios shimmer instead. Each voice is really two oscillators a few cents apart, and that tiny gap is where the living, breathing beat comes from.

The four systems are different families of ratios: harmonic is the natural overtone series (lushest); just major is classic pure thirds and fifths; pythagorean stacks perfect fifths (bright, with edgy thirds); septimal brings in the 7th harmonic — the "blue", outside-the-piano flavour.

Copy & save patches

A patch is just your current settings (tuning, voices, motion, root). The presets are saved starting points — tap one and tweak. copy patch copies a link with your settings baked in; paste it into notes or a message, and opening that link starts the drone on exactly those settings. That is how you save a sound you like and share it — nothing lives on a server, the settings travel inside the link.

New in v2

space adds a generated concert-hall reverb. binaural puts a slow beat between your ears — the root tone is split hard left/right with a small Hz offset (headphones required; keep it under 8 Hz). ● record captures the live drone and saves a WAV when you stop — up to 15 minutes. .scl exports the current tuning as a Scala file, the standard microtonal format most soft-synths and some hardware can load. The sleep timer fades the drone out over the last 45 seconds and stops. Three new tunings: subharmonic (the undertone mirror — darker), 13-limit (xenharmonic shimmer), well-tuned (after La Monte Young's piano).

Tips

Headphones or a quiet room — the low end and the beating need space. For deep stillness: harmonic, 4–5 voices, motion low, root ~50. For strange: septimal with more motion. If audio ever stops on iPhone, tap the screen once — iOS occasionally pauses synthesised audio and a tap wakes it.
